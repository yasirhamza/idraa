What Idraa is

Idraa measures cyber risk in money, not in colours on a heat map. Instead of labelling a risk “high” or “red”, it estimates how much money you could lose in a year — and how much of that loss a security control would prevent.

That lets you answer a plain business question: is this control worth what it costs? For more on the underlying method, see FAIR methodology primer.

Roles

Idraa has four roles. Access is controlled by an admin when user accounts are created.

  • Viewer — read-only access to scenarios, analyses, and results, the same visibility as Reviewer today. Suitable for stakeholders who only need to see outputs.
  • Reviewer — read-only access to scenarios, analyses, and results. Suitable for stakeholders who need to review outputs without authoring changes.
  • Analyst — builds scenarios, runs analyses, and downloads reports. The primary day-to-day role for anyone doing risk quantification work.
  • Admin — manages user accounts, imports scenario and control library data, and configures organisation-level settings. Admins can also perform all analyst, reviewer, and viewer actions.

Signing in and account security

Idraa requires multi-factor authentication for every account. On your first login you will be guided to enroll a passkey, an authenticator app (TOTP), or both, plus a set of one-time recovery codes, before you can use the rest of the app. Manage your enrolled factors any time at Account & security. Sensitive actions, such as downloading reports or importing data, may occasionally prompt you to re-authenticate if your session isn’t recent — that step-up prompt is a security measure, not an error.

The scenario → analysis → report flow

Everything in Idraa follows the same three-step path:

  1. Build a scenario (Scenarios). A scenario is one way you could be harmed — for example, ransomware on your operational technology systems. You enter low–high estimates for threat frequency, vulnerability, and loss magnitude, and tick the controls that reduce it. Start from the Build a scenario guide for a step-by-step walkthrough.
  2. Run an analysis (Analyses → New). Run one scenario on its own, or several together to see your combined exposure. Idraa runs thousands of Monte Carlo simulations across your input ranges and returns a realistic spread of outcomes: the typical year, a bad year, and the rare disaster.
  3. Share the report. Download a one-click executive PDF to present to leadership or a risk committee. Charts and control ROI tables are included automatically.

That is the complete cycle. Add more scenarios over time to build a fuller picture of your organisation’s exposure.

Caveats — read this

  • It is a decision-support tool, not a crystal ball. The outputs are ranges of possibility, not predictions of what will happen.
  • Good inputs, good answers. Your estimates drive everything. If they are rough guesses, treat the output as a rough guide — then refine the estimates as you learn more.
  • The library values are starting points. Ready-made scenarios and controls are calibrated for a typical organisation in your industry. Review them for your own context before relying on them.
  • Results are ranges, not a budget line. Use them to compare options and set priorities, not as a guaranteed figure to put in a spreadsheet cell.
