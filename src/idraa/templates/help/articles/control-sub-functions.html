FAIR-CAM sub-functions

Every control is described by one or more sub-functions — the specific FAIR-CAM mechanism by which it changes risk. Sub-functions group into three families. The picker on the control form is organized the same way.

LEC — Loss Event Control

Prevents, detects, or responds to threat events.

  • Prevent: Avoidance, Deterrence, Resistance
  • Detect: Visibility, Monitoring, Recognition
  • Respond: Event termination, Resilience, Loss reduction

VMC — Variance Management Control

Reduces how often, and how far, controls drift from their intended state.

  • Prevent: Reduce change frequency, Reduce variance probability
  • Identify: Threat intelligence, Control monitoring
  • Correct: Treatment selection, Implementation

DSC — Decision Support Control

Improves the quality and timeliness of risk decisions (governance, awareness, reporting).

  • Prevent: Defined expectations, Communication, Ensure capability, Incentives, and Situational awareness — which subdivides into data (asset, threat, controls), analysis, and reporting
  • Identify: Misalignment detection

FAIR-CAM §5.3 also defines a virtual Correct misalignment function. It has no controls of its own (it is fulfilled by the LEC/VMC correction functions), so it is not a pickable sub-function.

Each assignment also carries a capability, coverage, and reliability value. See the methodology primer and Controls & overlays for how these reduce modeled risk.
