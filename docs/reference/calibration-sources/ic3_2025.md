# FBI IC3 2025 — Reference Data

**Source:** Federal Bureau of Investigation, Internet Crime Complaint Center,
"2025 Internet Crime Report" (published 2026).
[ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2025_IC3Report.pdf](https://www.ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2025_IC3Report.pdf)

**Population covered:** Self-reported complaints submitted via www.ic3.gov by
victims, witnesses, or third parties — consumers, small business, and some
larger orgs mixed together with no size filter. Complaints span more than 200
countries; US-centric in volume.

**Methodology summary:** Complaints span fraud, cyber-enabled crime, and
non-fraud cyber threats. Reported losses are direct financial losses as
stated by complainants; do NOT include lost productivity, business
interruption, recovery costs, or reputational damage. Loss figures are NOT
inflation-adjusted (in contrast to IRIS 2025, which publishes 2024-dollar
values). See methodology section pages 58–62 of the report.

**Why this is reference-only (not calibration):** Population is not enterprise
loss-events; mixes consumer victims with corporate reporters. Crime taxonomy
(BEC, romance scam, investment fraud) does not align with IRIS's incident-pattern
+ ATT&CK taxonomy. Use IRIS for FAIR calibration; use IC3 for cross-validation,
fraud-context sidebars, and overlay-multiplier sourcing.

Reported losses are direct financial losses as stated by complainants; do NOT include lost productivity, business interruption, or reputational damage. |

### Known biases

- Self-selection — only victims who choose to report are counted.
- US-centric — non-US complaints exist (200+ countries) but volume is dominated by US victims.
- Direct losses only — indirect impact and recovery costs not captured.
- No corporate-tier filter — consumers, small business, and large org reports mixed together.
- Granular crime taxonomy mixes attack types (BEC) with fraud schemes (romance scam) — not directly comparable to ATT&CK.
- Ransomware loss figures explicitly conservative — do not include lost business, wages, files, or third-party remediation.
- Reported losses are NOT inflation-adjusted (unlike IRIS 2025 which adjusts to 2024 dollars).
