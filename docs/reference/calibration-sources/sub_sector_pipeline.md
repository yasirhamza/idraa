## Methodology
Population baseline: NAICS-2 transportation sector (NAICS 48-49, encompassing pipeline transportation at NAICS 486 alongside truck, rail, air, water transport, warehousing, and courier services), assuming NO CI overlay applied (per spec §6.4 stacking discipline).

Frequency lift derivation:
Pipeline transportation sits within NAICS 48 transportation, a population dominated by truck/rail/air/courier orgs that face IT-centric (not OT-centric) cyber risk. Pipeline operators are a small fraction of NAICS-48 by org count but face concentrated nation-state and ransomware-actor interest: the May 2021 Colonial Pipeline ransomware drove the US federal government to issue the first-ever TSA pipeline cybersecurity directive within days, indicating regulator-perceived attack-rate concentration at the pipeline sub-sector. CISA Energy advisories tagged to pipeline-relevant products (Schneider Modicon, Emerson Ovation, GE Mark VIe, OSIsoft PI Server) increased materially in 2021–2024. The TSA SD-Pipeline directive sequence (May 2021, July 2021, multiple revisions through 2023) reflects an active threat landscape recognized by federal authorities. Compared to the NAICS-48 aggregate which is diluted by lower-targeting trucking/courier sub-sectors, pipelines sit ~2× the aggregate base rate. Locked frequency lift: `2.0×`, mid-range within the §10.4 plausible 1.8–2.5× window. Choice avoids the floor (which would undercount the post-Colonial regulatory-recognized concentration) and avoids the ceiling (which would require pipeline-only Cyentia or NetDiligence breakout this provenance does not have).

Magnitude lift derivation:
Pipeline magnitude tail is dominated by infrastructure-disruption operational-impact components that the NAICS-48 aggregate does not isolate. The single concrete dollar anchor is Colonial Pipeline 2021: $4.4M ransom (subsequently ~$2.3M recovered) plus an estimated $20–50M operational impact (industry-analyst range; no audited public figure published; range cited from contemporary press analysis). Beyond the dollar anchor, two qualitative magnifiers: (1) regulatory tail — TSA SD-Pipeline directives carry civil-penalty exposure for non-compliance, layered on top of any incident-driven regulatory-investigation costs; (2) cascading-disruption tail — Colonial's 6-day shutdown triggered fuel shortages across multiple US states, illustrating that pipeline disruption magnitude is not bounded by the operator's direct revenue loss. NetDiligence 2024 Energy Vertical claim severity p50 runs materially above the all-industry p50. The IRIS NAICS-48 aggregate magnitude is pulled toward IT-only trucking/courier incident dollars; pipelines sit substantially above. Locked magnitude lift: `3.0×`, mid-range within the §10.4 plausible 2.5–3.5× window. Rationale: above the floor (Colonial's $24–54M total realized loss range justifies more than a 2.5× lift over NAICS-48 aggregate) but below the ceiling (only one Colonial-class incident anchors the dollar tail; broader corpus would justify pushing toward 3.5×).

## Suggested multipliers
- frequency_multiplier: 2.0
- magnitude_multiplier: 3.0

## Known limitations
- The magnitude lift is anchored almost entirely to a single incident (Colonial Pipeline 2021) plus its operational-impact range from industry-analyst commentary. No audited financial-loss disclosure has been published for Colonial. A future Colonial-class incident with audited financials, or a TSA / GAO cross-incident loss aggregate, would materially strengthen the magnitude anchor.
- Pipeline transportation (NAICS 486) does not appear as a publicly broken-out IRIS sub-sector; the 2.0× frequency lift interpolates from CISA advisory volume share, TSA directive-issuance cadence, and the post-Colonial regulator-recognition signal. Direct empirical anchoring (a pipeline-specific Cyentia or NetDiligence breakout) is desired.
- Colonial 2021 was an IT-side ransomware incident (billing-system compromise) that triggered an OT-side precautionary shutdown — not a direct OT compromise. Conflating IT-ransomware and OT-attack frequency into a single 2-axis multiplier is a known imprecision; spec §11.2 forward-compat for a third (vulnerability) axis.
- Stacking concern: the existing CI overlay (`critical_infrastructure`, freq 1.4× mag 2.0×) was calibrated against a population that included CI-designated pipelines. Multiplicative composition (combined freq 2.8×, combined mag 6.0× over NAICS-2 baseline) double-counts a small portion. Mitigation: stacking-pin test + spec §11.1 recalibration commitment.
- The Colonial ransom figure ($4.4M) and the operational-impact range ($20–50M) are cited at face value; the operational-impact figure in particular is industry-analyst commentary rather than audited disclosure.
