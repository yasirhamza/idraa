# Verizon DBIR 2024 — Reference Data

**Source:** Verizon Business, "2024 Data Breach Investigations Report" (17th
edition), published May 2024. PDF: https://www.verizon.com/business/en-au/resources/reports/2024/dbir/2024-dbir-data-breach-investigations-report.pdf
(landing: https://www.verizon.com/business/resources/reports/dbir/). 100-page
paginated PDF; page numbers in this doc match the printed page numbers in the
report (PDF page = printed page for this edition).

**Population covered:** Global cybersecurity incidents and confirmed data
breaches contributed by ~90+ partner organizations (CSIRTs, MSSPs, insurers,
breach-notification registries, law enforcement, Verizon's own VTRAC).
30,458 incidents and 10,626 confirmed breaches across 94 countries, classified
into eight VERIS Incident Classification Patterns (System Intrusion, Social
Engineering, Basic Web Application Attacks, Miscellaneous Errors, Denial of
Service, Lost and Stolen Assets, Privilege Misuse, Everything Else; see p29
Table 1).

**Methodology summary:** Sample-based, contributor-weighted. Counts represent
breaches observed by contributors in the November 1, 2022 to October 31, 2023
window (p6 "About the 2024 DBIR incident dataset"). Mandatory-breach-disclosure
contributors materially expanded the Internal-actor and Miscellaneous-Errors
sample versus 2023 (p15). Per-figure n is annotated in caption. Statistical
significance markers (the "whiskers" on bar charts) carry through all figures.
Survival-analysis methodology for vulnerability remediation (Figure 19, p21)
sourced from CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog combined with
contributor patch-management telemetry; methodology footnote 36 cites Jay Jacobs
(Cyentia) on MTTR caveats.

**Why this is calibration-primary (not reference-only):** The CISA KEV survival
analysis at Figure 19 (p21) publishes a directly citable population-level
remediation median (55 days at 50% unremediated) that is the canonical input
for `VMC_CORR_IMPLEMENTATION` τ. This source is also referenced for
benchmark-doc breakouts on vulnerability remediation speed (CISA KEV vs
non-CISA-KEV; Figure 20, p22). Caveat: DBIR does NOT publish time-to-discover
by attack vector or time-to-contain by industry in this edition (see negative
outcomes below) — those metrics route to IBM CODB 2024 instead.

## Patch deployment SLA (for VMC_CORR_IMPLEMENTATION)

**Figure 19 (p21), "Survival analysis of CISA KEV vulnerabilities" — the
primary citation.**

| Days since patch availability | % CISA KEV vulns still unremediated | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 30 | 85% | p21 Figure 19 annotation |
| 55 | **50% (median to remediate)** | p21 Figure 19 annotation |
| 60 | 47% | p21 Figure 19 annotation |
| 180 | 20% | p21 Figure 19 annotation |
| 365 | 8% | p21 Figure 19 annotation |

**Direct quote, p21 right column:**

> "CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog, (arguably an area of
> priority focus in vulnerability management), we found that it takes around
> 55 days to remediate 50% of those critical vulnerabilities once their
> patches are available. As Figure 19 demonstrates, the patching doesn't seem
> to start picking up until after the 30-day mark, and by the end of a whole
> year, around 8% of them are still open."

> "[E]nterprise patch management cycles usually stabilize around 30 to 60 days
> as the viable target, with maybe a 15-day target for critical vulnerability
> patching. Sadly, this does not seem to keep pace with the growing speed of
> threat actor scanning and exploitation of vulnerabilities."

**Re-verification of current `VMC_CORR_IMPLEMENTATION` τ:** The code comment
at `fair_cam/calibration/elapsed_time_taus.py:58-60` reads:

```
# 30-day patch deployment SLA (Verizon DBIR 2024 surveyed-orgs median).
# τ = 30 / ln(2) ≈ 43. Methodology doc cites exact DBIR page.
FairCamSubFunction.VMC_CORR_IMPLEMENTATION: 43.0,
```

**This comment is SUPERSEDED by direct page-citation re-verification:** the
DBIR 2024 *median* time to remediate 50% of CISA KEV vulnerabilities is **55
days, not 30**. The 30-day figure cited in the existing comment is the
enterprise patch-cycle *target* (narrative on p21 right column), not the
observed median. At τ = 55 / ln(2), the recalibrated value would be **≈ 79.3**,
materially larger than the current 43.

DBIR also surfaces a more aggressive **15-day critical-vuln target** (p21
narrative), which would yield τ ≈ 21.6 if v3 elects to model the
critical-CVE-only cohort. The plan-doc (`docs/plans/2026-05-15-issue-131-tau-calibration-design.md`
line 534) sketches a 7–14 day overlay for "EDR patch SLA per surveyed cohort"
that aligns with the 15-day critical target rather than the 55-day CISA-KEV
median.

**Implications:**
1. The canonical τ for `VMC_CORR_IMPLEMENTATION` should be updated to
   τ = 55 / ln(2) ≈ **79.3** (rounded) on the CISA-KEV median.
2. An overlay layer (per CLAUDE.md "Layered override beats canonical CRUD")
   should model the 15-day critical-vuln target as a per-org multiplicative
   delta (`critical_patch_sla_overlay`) yielding τ ≈ 21.6 for orgs with
   measured critical-patch programs.
3. The DBIR-line italics in the current comment ("30-day patch deployment
   SLA … surveyed-orgs median") cannot be substantiated against this page-cited
   source. Either the original commenter conflated *target* with *median*, or
   they cited a different DBIR edition. PR μ.1 backtest fixtures will need
   re-pinning if τ changes.

## Known anomalies / errata

- **Figure 19 axis label.** The Y-axis is labeled "Overall survival
  probability" — meaning the percentage of vulnerabilities still unpatched
  (still "surviving" against remediation), not the percentage successfully
  patched. Inverted readings ("85% of vulns remediated at 30 days") have
  appeared in third-party summaries; always confirm direction from Figure 19
  annotations.
- **30-day figure conflation.** Multiple secondary write-ups (industry blog
  recaps, vendor marketing) cite "30 days" as the DBIR's patch-SLA median.
  This is incorrect — 30 days is the start of meaningful patching activity
  per the narrative (p21 right column: "patching doesn't seem to start
  picking up until after the 30-day mark"), and the 50%-remediation point
  occurs at 55 days. The previous comment in `elapsed_time_taus.py:58-60`
  appears to have inherited this error.
- **Sample-bias caveat.** DBIR explicitly warns (p11, p56) that
  contributor-set turnover affects year-over-year comparability. The Internal-
  actor share jump from 20% (2023) to 35% (2024) is attributed primarily to
  new mandatory-breach-disclosure contributors, not to a real-world increase
  in insider threats (p15).
- **MOVEit dominance.** 1,567 of the 2024 breach notifications are MOVEit-
  related (p21, p34 Figure 32). This single zero-day campaign skews the
  Education / Finance / Information industry breakdowns for FY2024 and the
  System Intrusion pattern's distribution.
- **VPN/edge-appliance commentary is qualitative.** The "VPN as exploit
  vector" discussion on p12 and the "no figure for time-to-patch VPNs
  specifically" gap means VPN-specific τ-calibration is not extractable
  from this edition; only the aggregate CISA KEV curve is.
