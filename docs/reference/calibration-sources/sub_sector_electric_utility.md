## Methodology
Population baseline: NAICS-2 utilities sector (NAICS 22, encompassing electric power generation/transmission/distribution at NAICS 221, plus natural gas distribution, water utilities, sewage and waste), assuming NO CI overlay applied (per spec §6.4 stacking discipline).

Frequency lift derivation:
The IRIS 2025 NAICS-2 utilities baseline `p_annual ≈ 0.045` aggregates electric power, natural gas distribution, water/sewage, and waste management. Electric utilities face a documented nation-state-actor concentration that the aggregate underweights. Three quantitative anchors: (1) BlackEnergy 2015 plus Industroyer 2016 affected ~225,000 customers each in Ukraine — two distinct grid-disruption incidents in 18 months in a single national grid; (2) Industroyer2 2022 was a third attempted incident in the same theater within 7 years; (3) CISA ICS Advisory volume tagged "Energy" with electric-grid product mentions accounts for the largest single sub-vertical bucket in 2023 and 2024 advisory issuances. NERC E-ISAC reports a sustained year-over-year increase in nation-state reconnaissance against bulk-electric-system entities. Compared to the utilities aggregate which is diluted by lower-targeting water/waste sub-sectors, electric utilities sit ~2× the aggregate base rate. Locked frequency lift: `2.0×`, mid-range within the §10.4 plausible 1.5–2.5× window. The choice avoids the floor (which would undercount the documented nation-state concentration) and avoids the ceiling (which would require an electric-only Cyentia breakout this provenance does not have).

Magnitude lift derivation:
Electric-utility magnitude tail is dominated by grid-disruption operational-impact components that the NAICS-22 aggregate does not isolate. Concrete anchors: (1) Ukraine 2015/2016 outages affected ~225,000 customers each for 1–6 hours; even at conservative public-cost-of-outage figures (~$2–5/customer-hour for residential, materially higher for industrial/commercial), each incident's societal-impact magnitude reaches into the tens of millions; (2) Industroyer2 2022 prevented at the substation level had it succeeded would have affected millions; (3) NERC CIP regulatory penalty schedule alone reaches $1.4M/day per violation post-2020 schedule. NetDiligence 2024 utility/energy claims trend p75–p90 of all-industry severity. The IRIS NAICS-22 aggregate magnitude — pulled toward the lower-tail water/waste sub-segments — understates electric-grid magnitude tail materially. Locked magnitude lift: `2.7×`, mid-range within the §10.4 plausible 2.0–3.5× window. Rationale: above the floor (Ukraine-class operational-impact and NERC-CIP regulatory tail justify more than a 2× lift) but below the ceiling (no audited US grid incident has yet produced a Colonial-class disclosed dollar figure).

## Known limitations
- The Industroyer/Industroyer2/BlackEnergy quantitative anchors are Ukraine-grid incidents; US-grid incidents of comparable magnitude have not been publicly documented. Generalizing Ukraine-grid base-rate to US-grid posture is a known extrapolation. Mitigation: anchored to nation-state-targeting *concentration* (which is grid-class-invariant) rather than to incident frequency directly.
- Customer-outage-cost figures used in magnitude derivation are public utility commission planning estimates, not realized incident-loss disclosures. No US electric utility has publicly disclosed a Colonial Pipeline-equivalent realized financial loss for a successful cyber-physical incident. The magnitude lift therefore anchors to societal-impact + NERC CIP regulatory tail, not to a single audited incident-loss figure.
- IRIS 2025 NAICS-22 aggregate dilutes electric-utility signal with water/waste; the 2.0× frequency lift assumes the dilution factor approximates the inverse of electric utilities' fraction of NAICS-22 by org count. A future IRIS sub-sector breakout would supersede this assumption.
- Stacking concern: the existing CI overlay (`critical_infrastructure`, freq 1.4× mag 2.0×) was calibrated against a population that included CI-designated electric utilities. Multiplicative composition (combined freq 2.8×, combined mag 5.4× over NAICS-2 baseline) double-counts a small portion. Mitigation: stacking-pin test + spec §11.1 recalibration commitment.
- 2-axis multiplier conflates OT vulnerability into frequency lift; spec §11.2 forward-compat for third axis.
