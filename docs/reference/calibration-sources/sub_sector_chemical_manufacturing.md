## Methodology
Population baseline: NAICS-2 manufacturing sector (NAICS 31-33, encompassing food, textile, wood, paper, chemical at NAICS 325, plastics, primary metals, fabricated metals, machinery, electronics, transportation equipment, furniture, miscellaneous), assuming NO CI overlay applied (per spec §6.4 stacking discipline).

Frequency lift derivation:
NAICS-2 manufacturing has the highest IRIS 2025 baseline frequency among the major sectors at `p_annual ≈ 0.112`, dominated by ransomware targeting across all manufacturing sub-sectors. Chemical manufacturing (NAICS 325) within the aggregate carries documented additional risk vectors: (1) nation-state targeting (Bayer 2018 WINNTI espionage); (2) safety-system attack class (Triton 2017 at a petrochemical SIS); (3) high OT-IT convergence (continuous-process plants with high cost-of-shutdown). However, the *aggregate* manufacturing baseline already incorporates substantial OT/ransomware signal — meaning the marginal lift of chemical-over-aggregate is moderate, not extreme. The Cyentia "Headlines to Histograms" October 2025 caution explicitly addresses media-buzz vs base-rate over-attribution for manufacturing; this provenance heeds that caution by selecting toward the lower end of the §10.4 plausible range. Locked frequency lift: `1.7×`, mid-range within the §10.4 plausible 1.5–2.0× window. Choice deliberately avoids the ceiling, where over-multiplying a manufacturing baseline that is already the highest-frequency NAICS-2 baseline would produce implausibly high `p_annual` values for chemical orgs.

Magnitude lift derivation:
Chemical manufacturing magnitude tail is anchored by two concrete realized incidents: (1) Norsk Hydro LockerGoga 2019 — Q1 2019 disclosed NOK 300–350M (~$33–38M); Q2-only disclosed NOK 250–300M; cumulative NOK 550–650M (~$64–76M USD) per Hydro Q3-2019 financial results (Q3 PDF `id=252245` — reachability confirmed via web search only, NOT direct-fetched); includes forensic, restoration, and BI components with multi-plant manual-operations period; aluminum/light-metals (process-manufacturing-adjacent), used as a directly-applicable magnitude anchor; (2) JBS Foods 2021 — $11M disclosed ransom plus production-halt across multiple international plants, food manufacturing (process-manufacturing-adjacent), used as a secondary anchor. Production-halt loss component dominates the magnitude tail for continuous-process chemical plants where restart costs (specification-out-of-tolerance batches, catalyst replacement, regulatory re-certification of safety-systems) drag well beyond the IT-restoration cost. NetDiligence 2024 manufacturing claims trend shows elevated BI fraction. The IRIS NAICS-31-33 aggregate magnitude — pulled by lower-OT discrete-manufacturing (electronics, fabricated metals) — understates chemical-process magnitude tail. Locked magnitude lift: `2.5×`, mid-range within the §10.4 plausible 2.0–3.0× window. Rationale: above the floor (Norsk Hydro's Q1+Q2 cumulative ~$64–76M USD loss anchors more than a 2× lift over NAICS-31-33 aggregate magnitude) but below the ceiling (no audited Triton-class realized chemical-sector dollar figure exists; the safety-system tail is potential, not realized).

## Known limitations
- Chemical manufacturing (NAICS 325) is not publicly broken out by IRIS or by Verizon DBIR; the lift interpolates from process-manufacturing-adjacent anchors (Norsk Hydro aluminum, JBS food, Triton petrochemical) rather than from a chemical-sub-sector-specific empirical population. A future Cyentia or NetDiligence chemical-sub-sector breakout would directly re-anchor.
- Norsk Hydro is technically NAICS 331 (primary metals), not NAICS 325 (chemicals). It is used as a process-manufacturing-class anchor due to operational similarities (continuous-process plants, OT/IT convergence, multi-plant footprint). A direct NAICS-325 anchor of comparable magnitude would supersede.
- CFATS regulatory baseline lapsed in July 2023 (statutory authority not renewed by Congress). The chemical sub-sector currently lacks the pipeline-style mandatory cybersecurity-directive regime that elevates regulatory-tail magnitude for pipelines. The magnitude lift therefore does *not* incorporate a CFATS-equivalent regulatory tail; if Congress restores or expands CFATS-equivalent authority, the magnitude lift may need re-anchoring upward.
- Cyentia's October 2025 "Headlines to Histograms" caution against manufacturing media-buzz over-weighting is explicitly applied; the multiplier sits below the §10.4 ceiling rather than at it. Reviewers reconsidering the multiplier should re-read that caution before pushing values upward.
- Stacking concern: the existing CI overlay (`critical_infrastructure`, freq 1.4× mag 2.0×) was calibrated against a population that included CI-designated chemical facilities (CFATS-era data). Multiplicative composition (combined freq 2.38×, combined mag 5.0× over NAICS-2 baseline) double-counts a small portion. Mitigation: stacking-pin test + spec §11.1 recalibration commitment.
- 2-axis multiplier conflates higher OT vulnerability into the frequency lift; spec §11.2 forward-compat for third axis. Note also that `process_manufacturing` ships in PR η as a v3 enum value with NO fair_cam multiplier (per spec §3.2) — chemical_manufacturing is the closest related catalog entry but is not a substitute for a direct process_manufacturing multiplier.
