## Methodology
Population baseline: NAICS-2 mining sector (NAICS 21, dominated by Oil & Gas Extraction at NAICS 211 plus support activities at NAICS 213), assuming NO CI overlay applied (per spec §6.4 stacking discipline).

Frequency lift derivation:
The IRIS 2025 NAICS-2 mining baseline `p_annual ≈ 0.019` aggregates oil & gas extraction with hard-rock mining and quarrying. Hard-rock mining and quarrying carry comparatively low cyber-physical risk (limited OT-IT convergence, limited nation-state interest, limited public-disruption blast radius). Oil & gas dominates the cyber-incident signal within NAICS 21. CISA ICS Advisories filtered to the Energy sector consistently rank as the highest-volume sub-sector annually (Energy advisories number in the dozens per year while Mining/Metals advisories number in the low single digits). Verizon DBIR 2024 NAICS-21 panel shows ransomware and espionage motives both elevated for the energy slice. Combined with documented nation-state targeting (Shamoon 2012, Triton 2017) and ransomware payloads (Colonial 2021), an effective annual incident probability for oil & gas of `~0.04–0.06` is defensible — implying a frequency lift of `2.0–3.0×` over the NAICS-2 aggregate. Mid-range `2.5×` is the locked value: above the floor (which would understate the documented nation-state targeting concentration) but not at the ceiling (which would require a dedicated Cyentia OT-panel breakout this provenance does not have).

Magnitude lift derivation:
Magnitude lift is anchored to documented incident loss curves rather than to NAICS-21 aggregate figures, because the NAICS-21 aggregate is dominated by lower-magnitude IT-only incidents at hard-rock mining orgs. Three concrete anchors: (1) Saudi Aramco Shamoon 2012 — ~$15M direct IT remediation excluding production-IT downtime cost; (2) Colonial Pipeline 2021 — $4.4M ransom plus an estimated $20–50M operational impact range from regional fuel-supply disruption (no audited figure published; range cited from contemporary industry analyst commentary); (3) Triton 2017 — no realized loss figure but established the safety-system attack class with potential plant-loss-of-life severity. NetDiligence 2024 Energy Vertical claim severity p50 runs materially above the all-industry p50 (the report family attests this trend without specifying a single year-locked dollar figure for re-citation here). Loss tail is dominated by production-halt + safety-system incidents that the NAICS-21 aggregate underweights. A magnitude lift of `3.0×` over NAICS-2 mining baseline is mid-range within the §10.4 plausible 2.5–4.0× window, anchored to the Colonial-class operational-impact tail and the Shamoon-class IT-only multi-million tail.

## Suggested multipliers
- frequency_multiplier: 2.5
- magnitude_multiplier: 3.0

## Known limitations
- IRIS 2025 does not publish an oil-and-gas-specific frequency breakout publicly; the 2.5× lift interpolates from CISA advisory volume share within NAICS-21 and from Verizon DBIR mining/energy targeting trends. A future Cyentia OT-panel publication or an explicit IRIS sub-sector breakout would re-anchor this multiplier directly.
- Colonial Pipeline operational-impact range ($20–50M) is industry-analyst commentary, not an audited disclosure. Magnitude lift incorporates this range as an order-of-magnitude anchor, not as a precise empirical mean. Re-anchoring against a Colonial-class incident with audited financials (if ever disclosed) is desirable.
- Shamoon 2012 is a 14-year-old incident; threat landscape and IT/OT segmentation posture have evolved. Treated as a tail-event reference, not a base-rate input.
- Stacking concern: the existing CI overlay (`critical_infrastructure`, freq 1.4× mag 2.0×) was calibrated against a population that included CI-designated oil & gas orgs. Multiplicative composition with the oil_and_gas sub-sector multiplier (combined freq 3.5×, combined mag 6.0× over NAICS-2 baseline) double-counts a small portion of oil-and-gas-specific lift implicit in the CI overlay's calibration population. Mitigation: stacking-pin test makes the combined factor visible at review; recalibration of the CI overlay against a non-sub-sector population is committed in spec §11.1.
- 2-axis multiplier conflates higher OT vulnerability into the frequency lift; oil & gas objectively has higher *vulnerability* (legacy industrial protocols, weak segmentation), not just higher attack frequency. Forward-compat for a third axis is noted in spec §11.2.
