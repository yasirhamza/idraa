# FAIR Institute IRIS 2024

**Source:** Cyentia Institute, *Information Risk Insights Study — Ransomware*
(published 2024-08-05, sponsored by CISA).
[cyentia.com/press-release-iris_ransomware/](https://www.cyentia.com/press-release-iris_ransomware/) ·
[cyentia.com/category/iris/](https://www.cyentia.com/category/iris/)

**Population covered (2024 sub-study):** ~14,000 documented ransomware
incidents over a five-year window (~2019–2024), drawn from Cyentia's
Advisen/Zywave-derived corpus enriched with classification models. The
sub-study explicitly excludes non-ransomware incident patterns.

**Methodology summary (publicly disclosed):** Cyentia analyzed a five-year
retrospective of documented ransomware incidents to produce sector-share
and incident-rate metrics. Specific methodology details (loss-distribution
estimation, sample-weighting scheme, ATT&CK mapping for ransomware) are not
disclosed in the publicly visible preview — only headlines are published.

**Why this is reference-only (not calibration):** The 2024 IRIS-series
output is a ransomware-only sub-study; the publicly visible content does
not publish τ-relevant temporal metrics (time-to-detect, time-to-contain,
dwell time, encryption time, ransom-negotiation time, time-to-recovery).
No public IRIS source from 2024 informs FAIR-CAM `ELAPSED_TIME`
calibration. Per design doc §2 (`docs/plans/2026-05-15-issue-131-tau-
calibration-design.md`), the deeper IRIS report — when and if it
becomes accessible — is **deferred for future per-org override
calibration**, not phase-1 canonical τ.

## Critical disambiguation: there is no "IRIS 2024" main report

Industry references to "IRIS 2024" almost always mean one of three things:

1. **The IRIS Ransomware sub-study** (Cyentia + CISA, 2024-08-05) — a
   ransomware-only deep-dive. This is the *actual* 2024 publication.
2. **The IRIS 2022 main report** — Cyentia's most recent general-purpose
   IRIS through mid-2025, often cited as the "current IRIS" through 2024.
   IRIS 2022 itself is **publicly downloadable** as a PDF; numbers cited
   downstream often originate here, not from any 2024 release.
3. **The FAIR Institute 2024 Cybersecurity Risk Report** (October 2023
   publication date on the FAIR Institute side, sponsored by EY + Safe
   Security) — an *adjacent* FAIR Institute publication that re-uses
   Cyentia/IRIS findings but is a separate document. Often confused with
   "IRIS 2024" in press copy.

The follow-on **IRIS 2025** main report (released 2025-06-13, "It's About
Time" — 15 years / 77,000 events / $57B losses corpus) is publicly
downloadable and is already represented in this repo as
`fair_cam.data.iris_2025` plus
[`iris-vs-ic3-reconciliation.md`](./iris-vs-ic3-reconciliation.md).

**Practical implication for issue #131:** Phase-1 canonical τ calibration
does **not** depend on any IRIS 2024 source. The IRIS Ransomware sub-study
contributes incident-rate context, not τ. IRIS 2025 (when its τ-relevant
content is mined) and the future deep IRIS report are tracked as **future
per-org override** sources per design §2.

## Inaccessible: member-only / paywalled sections

Per design doc §2 — "Accept the paywall wall — deep sections documented as
'deferred for future per-org override calibration.'" The following IRIS
2024 (Ransomware sub-study) content is **not extractable** from the public
preview and is deferred:

- **Full report PDF** — Cyentia gates the full IRIS Ransomware report
  behind a request-the-report form / embargoed-media distribution. The
  press release alone is publicly visible.
- **Loss-distribution percentiles** by sector for ransomware specifically
  (p50 / p75 / p90 / p95 / p99) — not in public preview.
- **ATT&CK technique frequency** within ransomware kill chains — not in
  public preview.
- **Time-to-detect / time-to-contain / dwell time** specific to ransomware
  incidents — not in public preview. This is the explicit τ-relevant gap.
- **Per-sub-sector ransomware rates** beyond the four published industry
  shares (Manufacturing 51%, Financial 15%, plus enterprise revenue tiers).
  E.g., chemical / oil-and-gas / water-utility / electric-utility sub-sector
  rates are not published.
- **Ransom-payment behaviour** — fraction of victims who pay, ransom-size
  distribution, post-payment recovery rates — not in public preview.
- **Geographic breakdown** beyond US-vs-international.
- **Methodology appendix** — sample construction, weighting, exclusion
  criteria, classification model accuracy — not in public preview.

Additionally, FAIR Institute member-only resources that **may** contain
adjacent IRIS-derived τ context but are not accessible without
membership:

- FAIR Institute Benchmark Report (gated copy via the FAIR Institute site).
- Risk Retina® per-sector reports (Cyentia commercial product; partial
  free release for Healthcare, others gated).
- FAIR Institute Body of Knowledge — analyst's guide to data sources
  (publicly downloadable PDF — confirmed accessible at
  `fairinstitute.org/hubfs/FAIR%20CRM%20Body%20of%20Knowledge/...` but
  PDF text extraction returned encoded streams; manual review required
  for τ-relevant content).

## Known anomalies / errata

- **"IRIS 2024" is not a publication.** Vendor and press copy referring to
  "IRIS 2024" is almost always one of: (a) the August 2024 Ransomware
  sub-study, (b) the 2022 main report cited as "current through 2024," or
  (c) the FAIR Institute Cybersecurity Risk Report (October 2023 release
  date). Any τ value sourced to "IRIS 2024" without further specificity
  needs to be re-traced.
- **FAIR Institute primary URL** — the design-doc-suggested
  `https://www.fairinstitute.org/iris` returns HTTP 404 as of accessed
  date 2026-05-15. The current canonical landing is
  [Cyentia's IRIS archive](https://www.cyentia.com/category/iris/) on
  the publisher side, plus the FAIR Institute blog "Drawing FAIR
  Conclusions from Cyentia's IRIS" on the FAIR Institute side.
- **Five-year window endpoints** — Cyentia describes the IRIS Ransomware
  sub-study as a "five-year retrospective" but does not name explicit
  start/end years in the public preview. Inferred ~2019–2024 from
  publication date and IRIS-series cadence; not confirmed.
- **Cyentia / Advisen / Zywave provenance** — IRIS source corpus has been
  called "Advisen" (pre-2023) and "Zywave Cyber Loss Data" (2023+) after
  the corporate acquisition. The 2024 sub-study does not explicitly
  re-state provenance in the public preview.
